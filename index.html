About Me

From software engineering to solving real business problems

My path started in Software Engineering at the University of Malaysia Sabah, where I first learned how systems are built, tested and documented. That foundation gave me a habit I still rely on today: before fixing anything, understand how it actually works.

Working closely with developer teams as a Business Analyst taught me to think in systems — to see a vague business problem and translate it into requirements, user stories and flows that engineers can actually build from.

Later, supporting 50+ couriers and 100+ hubs daily as part of a fleet management team sharpened a different set of muscles entirely — calm communication, fast troubleshooting, and staying steady when escalations pile up across dozens of support groups at once.

Freelancing on the side gave me a third perspective: small businesses rarely need something flashy — they need a website or system that's clear, reliable and easy to maintain. That mix of system thinking, people skills and hands-on building is how I approach every project today.
